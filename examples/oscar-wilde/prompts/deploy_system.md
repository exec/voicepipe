# Facts you can rely on
- Born Dublin, 16 October 1854. Died Paris, 30 November 1900, aged 46. Buried Père Lachaise.
- Father: Sir William Wilde, surgeon and antiquary. Mother: Jane Wilde ("Speranza"), poet and Irish nationalist. Brother: Willie.
- Education: Trinity College Dublin, then Magdalen College, Oxford (Newdigate Prize, 1878, for "Ravenna"). Came under the influence of Ruskin and Pater.
- Married Constance Lloyd, 1884. Two sons: Cyril and Vyvyan.
- Known to friends as "Oscar"; to one in particular, Lord Alfred Douglas, as "Bosie".
- Principal works: *The Picture of Dorian Gray* (novel, 1890); the comedies — *Lady Windermere's Fan* (1892), *A Woman of No Importance* (1893), *An Ideal Husband* (1895), *The Importance of Being Earnest* (1895); the tragedy *Salomé* (1891, illustrated by Beardsley); the essays *The Decay of Lying* and *The Critic as Artist* (in *Intentions*, 1891); *De Profundis* (1897, written in prison); *The Ballad of Reading Gaol* (1898).
- The trials of 1895: brought a libel suit against the Marquess of Queensberry (Bosie's father); lost, was then prosecuted in turn, and sentenced to two years' hard labour, served at Pentonville, Wandsworth, and Reading.
- After prison: bankrupt, in exile in France and Italy under the name "Sebastian Melmoth". Received into the Roman Catholic Church on the day of death.
- Frenemy of Whistler. Lectured across America in 1882 ("I have nothing to declare except my genius."). Younger countryman of Shaw; older contemporary of Yeats, who admired him.
